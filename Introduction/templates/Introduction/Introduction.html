This is an experiment in the economics of decision-making. If you following the instructions carefully and make appropriate decisions, you can earn an appreciable amount of money, which will be paid to you shortly after the session.

Please read these instructions carefully! You will NOT be able to refer back to them after you leave this page.

In this experiment, your earnings will be denoted in Experimental Currency Units (ECU). 50 ECU are worth Â£1. After the experiment is over, we will convert the total of your earnings for the whole session into pounds.

This experiment is divided into 12 rounds. In each round, you will be presented with a series of tables containing ones and zeroes. Your task is to count and report the number of ones in the table. You will earn an income of 10 ECU for each table for which you report the correct number of ones. A typical table is shown here:

Each round lasts 40 seconds. At the end of the round, you will see the number of tables on which you reported the correct number of zeroes, and the total income you receive from these reports.

{% if session.config.tax_framing %} Your income is taxable and you will be required to fill in a tax return. The tax rate on your income is 25%. This means that for every 10 ECU you declare on the tax return, you will pay 2.50 ECU in tax. {% else %} You will be required to announce your amount of income. The amount of income you announce will attract a deduction of 25%. This means that for every 10 ECU you announce, you will pay 2.50 ECU in deductions. {% endif %}

{% if session.config.tax_framing %} Once you complete your tax form, the tax authority may choose to audit it. There is a 1 in 5 chance (or 20% probability) your tax form will be audited. {% else %} Once you complete your announcement, your announcement may be reviewed. There is a 1 in 5 chance (or 20% probability) your announcement will be reviewed. {% endif %}

{% if session.config.tax_framing %}

If you are NOT selected for tax audit: Your final earnings for the round will be equal to your income minus the tax paid based on the income you reported on your tax form.

If you ARE selected for tax audit:

  • If you reported your income accurately, then nothing further will happen; your earnings for the round will be the same as if you had not been audited.
  • If you are audited and if you reported less income than you actually earned, then you will be required to pay the extra amount of tax due to the authority. In addition, you will pay a fine of 1 ECU for each ECU of underpaid tax.
{% else %}

If you are NOT selected for review: Your final earnings for the round will be equal to your income minus the deductions based on the income you announced.

If you ARE selected for review:

  • If you announced your income accurately, then nothing further will happen; your earnings for the round will be the same as if you had not been reviewed.
  • If you are reviewed and if you announced less income than you actually earned, then you will be required to pay the extra amount of deductions due. In addition, you will pay a further deduction of 1 ECU for each ECU of underpaid deductions.
{% endif %}
