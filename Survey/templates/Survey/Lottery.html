In this part of the experimant, we will ask you to make a number of decisions. The decisions we will ask you to make will be choices between pairs of lotteries. There are 10 pairs of lotteries, as listed in this table.

Each lottery has two outcomes. The columns named Prob give the probability of each outcome of the draw, while the ECU columns indicate the value of the outcome.

For example, in Decision #1, picking Lottery A means you have a 10% chance of getting 40.00 ECU and a 90% chance of getting 32.00 ECU; picking Lottery B means you have a chance of getting 77.00 ECU and a 90% chance of getting 2.00 ECU.

Note that as you move down the table, the probability associated with the higher rises for both Lottery A and Lottery B. In fact, once you get to Decision 10, that higher-payoff outcome for each lottery will happen with certainty. Your choice in Decision 10 is therefore between 40 ECU for sure if you pick Lottery A or 77 ECU for sure if you pick Lottery B.

You must decide whether and when to switch from Lottery A to Lottery B. If you wish to play Lottery A in all Decisions, write 11 in the box provided. If you wish to play Lottery B in all Decisions, write 1 in the box.

Write a number between 2 and 10 in the box to switch to playing Lottery B starting with that Decision. For example, if you would like to play Decision A for Decisions 1 to 5 and B for Decisions 6 to 10, you should write 6 in the box.
