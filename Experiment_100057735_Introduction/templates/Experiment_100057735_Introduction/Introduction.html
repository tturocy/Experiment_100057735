This is an experiment in the economics of decision-making. If you following the instructions carefully and make appropriate decisions, you can earn an appreciable amount of money, which will be paid to you shortly after the session.

Please read these instructions carefully because you will NOT be able to refer back to them after you leave this page.

In this experiment, your earnings will be denoted in Experimental Currency Units (ECU). 50 ECU are worth Â£1. After the experiment is over, we will convert the total of your earnings for the whole session into pounds.

This part of the experiment is divided into 12 rounds. In each round, you will be presented with a series of tables containing ones and zeroes. Your task is to count and report the number of ones in the table. You will earn an income of 10 ECU for each table for which you report the correct number of zeroes.

Each round lasts 40 seconds. At the end of the round, you will see the number of tables on which you reported the correct number of zeroes, and the total income you receive from these reports.

Your income is taxable and you will be required to fill in a tax return. The tax rate on your income is 25%. This means that for every 10 ECU you earn, you must pay 2.5 ECU in tax.

All Tax Income will be provided to the NHS. We will send details on the donation to participants shortly after the completion of this series of experiments.

To fill in the tax return, you have simply to declare your income. Given the amount of income that you declare, the computer will automatically compute the total amount of tax you will be required to pay.

Once you complete your tax form, the tax authority may choose to audit it. There is a 1 in 5 chance (or 20% probability) your tax form will be audited.

If you are NOT selected for tax audit: Your final earnings for the experimental round will be equal to your income minus the tax paid based on the income you reported on your tax form.

If you ARE selected for tax audit:

  • If you reported your income accurately, then nothing further will happen; your earnings for the round will be the same as if you had not been audited.
  • If you are audited and if you reported less income than you actually earned, then you will be required to pay the extra amount of tax due to the authority. In addition, you will pay a fine of 1 ECU for each ECU of underpaid tax.

After the audit is completed, you will be presented with a screen that summarises the following:

  • How much income you earned by solving tables
  • The amount of income you reported to the tax authority
  • Whether you were audited or not by the tax authority
  • Your final income for the round.

Summary

In short, each round in this part of the experiment will consist of three stages:

STAGE 1: You earn income by solving tables.

STAGE 2: You declare your income to the tax authority.

STAGE 3: Your final earnings for the round depend on how your report your income and whether you were audited or not.
