Welcome to our experiment. Please read the instructions carefully because you will NOT be able to refer back to them. Your cash earnings in this experiment will depend on the decisions you take. It is therefore important that you understand the rules of the experiment.

In this experiment, your earnings will be denoted in Experimental Currency Units (ECU). 50 ECU are worth £1. After the experiment is over, we will convert the total of your earnings for the whole session into pounds and pay you through bank transfer.

This part of the experiment is divided into 12 rounds. In each round, you will have the opportunity to earn ECU by solving as many tables as possible within 40 seconds.

You will be presented with various tables including zeros and ones. You earn ECU for entering the number of ones within each table.

In each experimental round solve as many tables as you can within the time limit. You will earn 10 ECU for each table you solve.

After 40 seconds, the task ends and the computer will inform you of the total number of tables you had successfully solved as well as your total earnings from the task. This is your income from your task.

Your income is taxable and you will be required to fill in a tax return. The tax rate on your income is 25%. This means that for every 10 ECU you earn, you must pay 2.5 ECU in tax.

All Tax Income will be provided to the NHS. Evidence of donation will be emailed to participants shortly after all experiments are completed.

To fill in the tax return, you have simply to declare your earnings. Given the amount of earnings that you declare, the computer will automatically compute the total amount of tax you will be required to pay.

Once you complete your tax form, the tax authority may choose to audit it. There is a 1 in 5 chance (or 20% probability) you tax form will be audited.

If you are NOT selected for tax audit: Your final earnings for the experimental round will be equal to the amount of earnings you had made from the task minus the tax you had paid on the earnings you reported on your tax form.

If you ARE selected for tax audit:

•If you are audited and if you reported your income accurately, then nothing further will happen; your final earnings for the round will be the same as if you had not been audited.

•If you are audited and if you reported less income than you actually earned, then you will be required to pay the extra amount of tax due to the authority. In addition, you will pay a fine of 1 ECU for each ECU of underpaid tax.

Now let's go through a few hypothetical examples.

Your Income 10 ECU
Your Declared Income 10 ECU
Tax paid on declared income 2.50 ECU
Selected by Tax Authoritiies for Audit? Yes
Additional Tax Payable 0 ECU
Fine 0 ECU
END OF ROUND INCOME 7.50 ECU

EXAMPLE 2

Your Income 20 ECU
Your Declared Income 16 ECU
Tax paid on declared income 4 ECU
Selected by Tax Authoritiies for Audit? Yes
Additional Tax Payable 1 ECU
Fine 1 ECU
END OF ROUND INCOME 14 ECU

EXAMPLE 3

Your Income 16 ECU
Your Declared Income 10 ECU
Tax paid on declared income 2.50 ECU
Selected by Tax Authoritiies for Audit? No
Additional Tax Payable 0 ECU
Fine 0 ECU
END OF ROUND INCOME 13.50 ECU

After the audit is completed, you will be presented with a screen that summarises the following:

  • How much income you earned by solving tables
  • The amount of income you reported to the tax authority
  • Whether you were audited or not by the tax authority
  • Your final income for the round.

Summary

In short, each round in this part of the experiment will consist of three stages:

STAGE 1: You earn income by solving tables.

STAGE 2: You declare your income to the tax authority.

STAGE 3: You get your final earnings, which depend on how your report your income and whether you were audited or not.
